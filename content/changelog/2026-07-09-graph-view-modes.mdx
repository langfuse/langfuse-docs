The **graph view** turns an agent trace into a picture of what your agent did — nodes for steps, edges for how it moved between them. Over the last weeks we rebuilt it from the ground up, and it now ships with **two modes** so you can look at the same run two different ways.

## A rebuilt graph, on our own renderer

The graph is no longer a generic diagram drop-in. It now runs on our **own renderer**: a deterministic layout (built on ELK) drawn onto a stable, zoomable canvas. Practically, that means the graph lays out the *same way every time* you open a trace — no reshuffling between renders — and the viewport stays put as you pan and zoom, so you don't lose your place on a big agent. The layout is built to stay readable as traces grow.

That foundation is what made the next part possible.

## Two modes: Aggregated and Expanded

There's now an **Aggregated / Expanded** toggle in the top-left of the graph. It re-draws the same trace two ways — the difference is whether repeated work is grouped by name or shown call-by-call. Your choice is remembered across traces.

- **Aggregated** (the view you've had, now named) is a **compact overview**. Steps that share a name collapse into a single node with a counter — `retrieve_docs (3/3)` means it ran three times — and loops are drawn as cycles. It's the fastest way to see an agent's overall *shape* and complexity without every individual call on screen.

- **Expanded** shows the process **as it ran**. Every call is its own node, so three `litellm_request` calls are three distinct nodes, and loops unroll into an acyclic graph (a DAG) in execution order. It's the more literal view — closer to the trace tree — for following a specific run or pinning down exactly where something happened.

Neither is "the right one" — they answer different questions. Reach for Aggregated to understand an agent's structure at a glance, and Expanded to walk through a single execution step by step.

## Why

People liked the graph but told us the aggregated form could be hard to follow: pattern-grouping renders repeated calls as cycles, and not everyone reads a cyclic graph easily. Several asked for the full process as a straight DAG, in the order it ran, as an alternative — even if that overlaps with the tree view. That's exactly what Expanded is.

The graph view is in beta — see the [Agent Graphs docs](/docs/observability/features/agent-graphs) for how graphs appear and when to use each mode, and tell us what you'd like next.

You already filter the Observations table down to the exact rows you care about. Now you can see that same slice as a chart without leaving the page. A **Table | Chart** toggle in the toolbar turns the current view into a time series, and a **Visualize** panel picks how to plot it.

The chart runs on the same query as the table: the same filters, the same time range, the same data. You are not building a new query, you are looking at the one you already have from a different angle.

## Shape the chart

The **Visualize** panel defines the chart along four dimensions:

- **Chart type**: Line, Area, Bars, Ranked, Pie, or Number.
- **Metric**: Count, Latency, Cost, or Tokens.
- **Aggregation**: Count, Sum, Average, or a percentile such as p95, with the options depending on the metric.
- **Breakdown**: split by a dimension such as model, environment, or name, so each series is one value of that dimension.

"Count of events by model" and "p95 latency by model" are two selections in the same panel over the same filtered rows.

## Same filters, honestly

The chart reuses the table's filters. A few of them do not translate to an aggregated time series, such as filtering by score, metadata, or free-text search. Rather than silently drop those or hide the chart, Langfuse keeps the chart visible and deactivates just those filters in place, dimmed with a note that they still apply to the table. You always see the chart, and you always know exactly which of your filters it reflects.

## Add it to a dashboard

When a view is worth keeping, **Add to dashboard** saves the current chart, filters and all, as a widget on one of your custom dashboards. The exploratory chart you built in the table becomes a permanent tile you can arrange next to the rest.

## Why

The Observations table and custom dashboards were two separate places: one to find rows, one to plot them. This closes the gap. The fastest path from "these traces look off" to "here is the trend" is now a single toggle, and the same view that answered your question can graduate straight into a dashboard.
